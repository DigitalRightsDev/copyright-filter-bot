EU Copyright upload filter bot simulation

The simulation presents a mockup of how the copyright content upload filter of the EU copyright directive reform proposal would technically operate and how user content platforms will end up working like if it is approved.

Even though technical recognition could be achievable, a bot will never be able to identify legal uses of copyrighted content like quotations, parodies or critiques, heavily undermining our freedom of expression and information.

Tech

The simulation is completely front-end. A user interactive upload/database search/identify system would have required huge amounts of time and effort.

Only companies with massive resources can afford the development of this kind technologies. This results on giving US technological giants like Google or Facebook even more competitive advantage in front of smaller EU actors.

About Digital Rights Dev

The project goal is to construct tools and prototypes to help preserve citizens rights and freedoms in the digital environment and to give input and technical support in policy developments that have an impact on them.

Even though still in alpha, the project does its part to face of the emergency to stop the approval of the copyright upload filter.
